STLC Ladder Rules 2010

rev May 10



The principal of our rules:

Fair, fun, active & less administration.



Time & court

  1. Monday (7~11PM) is the official ladder game time, 3 courts will be reserved for ladder games.

  2. The Ladder games will start at MAY 11 and will be ended at SEPT 19.



Goal & basic rules

  1. A match between 2 teams/players is 8 games. If tied at 7-7 then the 8th game should be a 7 point tie breaker.

  2. The basic goal is try to be at the top level of the ladder. At the end of the season the winner will be awarded.

  3. On the ladder, a player/teams will be as either a 'challenger' or 'defender'; a challenger should challenge THE NEXT LEVEL ABOVE, a defender should accept a challenge from next lower level; the team/player at the top of the ladder will be always set to 'defender'.

  4. A 'special defender' can challenge up if no challenge received, otherwise it should accept challenge like a defender.

  5. A challenge will be launched from a lower level player/team to a higher level player/team. The winner will take the spot of higher level, and will be set to 'challenger', the loser will take the lower level spot, and will be set to 'defender';

  6. Defender must accept challenge if no other challenge on. If multiple challenges are sent to the same defender on the same day, the defender should accept the team/player which challenged earlier should be accepted.

  7. The challenger must provide balls that are in reasonable condition for the match.

  8. A player/team does not have to play more than one match per week.

  9. Tennis is fun game, please be generous, with good manners on the court and be honest to line calls, if you are not sure about the lines, do not call out.



Communication:

  1. Email should be the essential tool for challenging/responding ,It also will used as general proof. All email must c.c. ladder.stephen.leacock@gmail.com.

  2. The WINNER should always report the result using http://tennis-ladder.appspot.com/.within 2 days, special case, Loser can report as well.

  3. Whoever report the match must be careful since it's hard to reverse the result reported from the website. Abusively using the website will result in the team been disabled.



Initial game

A random draw will be performed at the start of season, and initial match will be arranged.

Ladder report

  1. The standing will be reflected on http://tennis-ladder.appspot.com/ right after a match been reported.



Defaulting:

The following situations will cause a default game:

  1. -If a defender failed to response a challenge in 5 days,or failed to meet challenge longer than 2 weeks since a challenge was sent, the defender will default to challenger.(The challenger is responsible to report the default)

  2. -If one team (say team A) is not available at the official ladder court time and both teams can not make other arrangement for the rest of the week, team A will be default to its opponent.

  3. -2 teams made a schedule, if one team (say team A) for any reason being late for more than 15 minutes, team A will be default to its opponent, any schedule changing without successfully communication between teams will assume the game still on.





Active & open ladder

  1. In order to keep the ladder active, after 2 days since last match, the 'CHALLENGER' or 'DEFENDER' status will be automatically 'switched' to 'special defender'; a 'special defender' can challenge up if no challenge received, otherwise it should accept challenge like a defender.

  2. This year we have many players interested in the ladder game, so we decided to give everyone a chance to play; the bottom of the ladder is open to anyone who is interested and qualified.



Foot Faults.

A player may warn an opponent that the opponent has committed a flagrant foot fault. If the foot faulting continues, the player may attempt to locate an official. If no official is available, the player may call flagrant foot faults. Compliance with the foot fault rule is very much a function of a player’s personal honor system. The plea that a Server should not be penalized because the server only just touched the line and did not rush the net is not acceptable. Habitual foot faulting, whether intentional or careless,is just as surely cheating as is making a deliberate bad line call.
